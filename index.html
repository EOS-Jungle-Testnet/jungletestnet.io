The Jungle Testnet

The Jungle is the place to test your software and infrastructure. It has served ass sandbox for many dApps development teams and EOS Block Producers since the very conception of the EOS Network. It played a cornerstone role in the EOS launch and now as the network matures and grows it provides a reliable and performant network for testing EOS based applications.

Your dApp's success is our number one priority.

Get Started

Follow these steps to test your decentralized dApp on the Jungle.

1. Create an Account

To create an EOS account on the Jungle TestNet visit jungle.cyrptolions.io/#account and choose a 12 character username. You can generate the EOS key pairs here.

2. Get Free Tokens

EOS Tokens entitle you to use network resources: RAM, CPU and Bandwidth. Through the faucet service you can get free tokens on the Jungle Tesnet for any account.

3. Deploy and Test

We have created this easy to follow video tutorial on how to deploy and test your smart contracts and dApps on the EOS Jungle TestNet. view video

TOOLS

Monitor

EOS Jungle Monitor

Faucet

Through the EOS Jungle Faucet service you get free tokens to host and use your smart contracts and decentralized applications.

Wallets

You can configure popular EOS wallets with your jungle accounts. Follow this video tutorial to configure Scatter for dApp testing.

APIs

You can choose from a myriad of API endpoints on the Jungle Blockchain. jungle.cryptolions.io/#apiendpoints.

Cleos

Cleos is the command interface developed by Block One to interact with EOSIO based blockchain nodes.

DApp Dev Env

EOS Local is docker based local development environment for building scalable EOS dApps.

Snaptshots

The Jungle Testnet snaphots allow you to spin up EOSIO nodes quickly without needing to replay the blockchian.
